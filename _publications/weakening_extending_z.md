By weakening an inference rule satisfied by logic $$daC$$, we define a new paraconsistent logic $$daC^{'}$$, which is weaker than logic $$\mathbb{Z}$$ and $$G^{'}3$$, enjoys properties presented in $$daC$$ like the substitution theorem, and possesses a strong negation which makes it suitable to express intutionism. Besides, $$daC^{'}$$ helps to understand the relationships among other logics, in particular $$daC$$, and $$PH1$$.
